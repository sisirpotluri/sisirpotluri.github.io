A Tale

I believe everyone should write a story. I wrote something as a tribute to my Indian heritage, and it might become something one day. So, here's my pitch.
In a tale of heroes and heroines who are neither beautifully ideal nor disgustingly evil, circumstance plays a cruel game that feasts on status, love, betrayal, greed, lust, and rage. No soul is left unscarred and no tear is left unavenged. It's a tale that attacks the fragile balance between fate and will. In the vermillion-colored dance of a lost princess, the thundering love between valiant warriors, the divine songs of childish worship, and the delicate lust of a mighty king, the tale reminds that the Sun sets only to rise again. It portrays the ruin that blooms from admiration, the permanence born from death, and the courage awakened from devastation. It is one story. It is everyone's story. In it lie all stories and in all lies its story.

This tale's world.
